42. Quality Control vs. Quality Selection 🔗

February 19, 2020
In which I distinguish quality control from quality selection, argue that creative stall recovery requires the counterintuitive move of lowering your standards and diving ‚Äî using the MCU's bet on second-string properties as proof ‚Äî and confess I'm trying to talk myself into exactly that.
🔗
Shifting from quality as a yield fraction of quantity (the high-grading intrinsic approach to quality) to quality as a controllable process outcome (six sigma) is a total valley of death. Creative work should be defined as that which cannot survive the transition.
🔗
To apply control control as opposed to quality selection (high grading) you need a formula to apply it to. When you don‚Äôt have one, but energy is falling faster than yield rate is rising, it is very tempting to invent one to keep absolute yields constant to pay the bills.
🔗
This is the moment of stall crisis for the process. To recover, you have to counter-intuitively five rather than climb. Ie lower the selection threshold for ‚Äúhigh grade‚Äù. In a way this is the opposite of forcing a formula and trying to faux-optimize (fauxtimize?) it.
🔗
Really hard to do because every instinct and cultivated taste will protest. You‚Äôre burning reputational capital (altitude) to get higher yield rate (velocity) to recover from the stall (critically low absolute yield)
🔗
Generally this also means reorienting what you‚Äôre solving for. Lowering quality without opening up objectives (changing flight plan = innovation) means the lowered quality is a deadweight loss.
🔗
This is probably a manifestation of the bias-variance tradeoff in disguise, but I‚Äôm feeling too lazy to work out how to connect the dots en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bias‚Äìvari‚Ä¶

You‚Äôre done as a live player the day you‚Äôre not willing to put out something shoddy by your standards. Consistent output and a consciously set subjective high minimum bar is inevitably also an unconsciously set low maximum bar. You‚Äôll never stall, but you‚Äôll cease to surprise.
🔗
I should name this. Yield-rate/next-peak tradeoff under fixed minimum quality. If you try to increase yield rate, next peak will be lower, and vice versa. Or more precisely, the next ‚Äúall time high‚Äù (ATH) will be a smaller improvement over last. Diminishing marginal altitude.
🔗
Even money doesn‚Äôt break this tradeoff, which is a rare failure of money to solve problems outside of death. Money allows you to add a new dimension (depth/difficulty) but not break basic yield-rate/next-peak tradeoff. The only way is to lower quality bar and open up direction.
🔗
MCU is a great example in high-money end. MCU lost top property (Spider-Man) to Sony due to bad contracts. So they dropped bar and made things work with second-string properties. When that had stall problems (Hulk 2, Thor 2), they dropped bar again (Guardians of Galaxy)! Gutsy.

Both diving to get out of a stall and going on rock-steady cruise control at an efficient altitude to ‚Äúcash out‚Äù usually require cash reserves or steady funding. Need money to reorient vs need money to go deep. Two ways to use $. Kinda like seed funding vs growth/scaling funding.

$ and creation interact differently in quality control (QC) vs quality selection (QS) modes.

In QC mode, you need $ during tooling period. Building infrastructure needed for low-variance steady output.

In QS mode, you need $ while retraining quality filter after a dive+reorient

In QC mode, you have a long period of no output to patch over because you‚Äôre building tooling. Capex. In writing, that‚Äôs reading, building and testing frameworks (=scaffolding/rigging) to hold production flows later, instrumentation (=edit pass filters) to check quality etc.

Or when you‚Äôre genuinely pushing hard limits. Apollo required BOTH insane levels of QC and the QS iteration. Takes lots of $$

Low QC/low QS: failing at play
Low QC/high QS: playing well
High QC/low QS: skilled craft work
High QC/High QS: pioneering

Semiconductors are a good example of top-right. Moore‚Äôs law = always trying to ride the edge of both high-QC design process and high-yield fab process. You want bug-free chips at high fab yield rates.
